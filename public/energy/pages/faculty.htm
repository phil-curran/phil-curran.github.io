Instructor

Alan Carroll

Professor, Department of Geoscience, University of Wisconsin - Madison

Alan Carroll is a geologist with over 30 years experience in academic research and the energy industry. He is currently a Professor at the University of Wisconsin-Madison, where he conducts research on sedimentary basins, and is internationally known for his contributions to the geology of ancient lakes and the tectonic evolution of western China. His teaching includes a popular Energy Resources course. He is a founding member of the Wisconsin Energy Institute, a member of the Nelson Institute for Environmental Studies, and Founding member of the Wisconsin Energy Institute and a member of the Nelson Institute for Environmental Studies and the Energy Analysis and Policy certificate program. He has also worked as an exploration geologist for Sohio Petroleum and as a petroleum geochemist for Exxon Production Research Company. He continues to maintain active contact with the industry through his consulting company, Geofuels LLC.

Dr. Carroll is a Fellow of the Geological Society of America, past Associate Editor of the American Association of Petroleum Geologists Bulletin, and member of the American Geophysical Union and Internal Association for Limnogeology. He holds degrees in geology from Carleton College, University of Michigan, and Stanford University. He is also an avid pilot, who enjoys cross-country air racing in an award-winning experimental airplane that he built himself.

http://geoscience.wisc.edu/geoscience/people/faculty/alan-carroll/

Guest Presenters

Tim Carr

Marshal Miller Professor of Geology, West Virginia University

Professor Carr is an internationally recognized expert on unconventional oil and gas resources and CO2 storage and utilization in North America, Europe, Middle East and Asia. He teaches shale-gas and oil short courses and works with the U.S. Department of State to assist countries in developing the expertise to establish and regulate unconventional resources. He is also the principal investigator for a West Virginia University research project that will create the first-ever laboratory for the long-term study of shale-gas resources that looks at the full life cycle of a well – from drilling to completion to production. He is a founding member and Past-President of the Council of Energy Research and Education Leaders. Tim spends his leisure time working on his cabin, and kayaking and fishing in the Puget Sound.

http://geology.wvu.edu/people/faculty/tim-carr

 

Michael Corradini

Director of the Wisconsin Energy Institute

Professor Corradini is a nuclear engineer with expertise related to reactor operation and safety, nuclear waste waste disposal. He has received numerous awards and honors during his career, and is a prominent leader among the nuclear engineering community. He is a member of the National Academy of Engineering, and is a past president of the American Nuclear Society. He has also served on many high-profile committees and review boards, including the U.S. Department of Energy Nuclear Energy Advisory Committee, the U.S. NRC Advisory Committee on Reactor Safeguards, and U.S. Nuclear Waste Technical Review Board. He co-chaired the American Nuclear Society’s Special Committee on Fukushima.

Shaun Marcot

Assistant Professor, Department of Geoscience, University of Wisconsin - Madison

Shaun Marcott is a glacial geologist and paleoclimatologists in the Department of Geoscience at the University of Wisconsin-Madison. His research is focused on late Quaternary climate change and dating of glacial landscapes to better understand the interplay between climate and glaciers of the past in order to place modern climate-glacier interactions into a prehistorical context for understanding future changes. With a broad background in geology, oceanography, and mathematics he teaches a wide variety of subjects including introductory courses in oceanography and geology and upper level courses in paleoclimatology and glacial geology. He holds degrees in geology from Oregon State and Portland State Universities and is a native Alaskan with family members and friends in the energy industry. In addition to his research, he enjoys spending time in the outdoors with his aging golden retriever and his growing family.

http://geoscience.wisc.edu/geoscience/people/faculty/shaun-marcott/

 

Tina Nielsen

Associate Director of the Great Lakes Bioenergy Resource Center - Madison, Wisconsin

Tina Nielsen has held positions focused on energy since 2006. Dr. Nielsen joined the Great Lakes Bioenergy Research Center (GLBRC) as its Associate Director in 2014. Housed at the University of Wisconsin-Madison, GLBRC is one of three Department of Energy funded centers dedicated to the advancement of cellulosic biofuels through basic science research. Being on the leadership team of this large research center requires a balance between internal administrative responsibilities with external government and industry relations all while keeping science at the forefront.

Prior to joining the GLBRC, Dr. Nielsen spent over 8 years in the energy industry primarily in a geoscience role. As a deep-water sedimentologist at BP and Conoco-Phillips, she has worked exploration and production both domestically (Gulf of Mexico and on-shore US) and internationally (Brazil). In addition to her technical responsibilities, Dr. Nielsen was very involved in university relations and recruiting leading both the University of Michigan and University of Wisconsin geoscience activities during her tenure. Prior to returning back to university for her graduate degrees, Tina was a science teacher at Marysville (MI) Intermediate School.

Tina holds a BS (’95), Secondary Teaching Certificate (’97) and MS (’03-Department of Geological Sciences) from the University of Michigan in addition to a PhD (’08-Department of Geology and Geophysics) from the University of Wisconsin-Madison. Her graduate research focused on marine sedimentology and geochemistry.

https://www.glbrc.org/about/tina-nielsen

Paul Meier

Associate Scientist at the Wisconsin Energy Institute - Madison, WI

Paul Meier, P.E., Ph.D. is an Associate Scientist at the Wisconsin Energy Institute at UW-Madison. His research uses models to simulate long-term supply and demand for electricity, transportation, and fuels. Recent and ongoing work has examined electric vehicles, advanced biofuels, natural gas trucking, renewables policy, as well as long-term projections for U.S. electricity supply. Meier is the developer of JuiceBox.org, a networked modeling platform where collaborators can construct virtual projects, test regional strategies, and share ideas within a national power-sector simulation.

https://energy.wisc.edu/about/people/meier
